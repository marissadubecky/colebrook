History

Nestled in the northwest corner of Connecticut, Colebrook is home to around 1,500 people.

It was the last town in colonial Connecticut to be settled, officially established in October 1779 and named after a town in Devonshire, England.

----------***----------

Late historian Bob Grigg spent the latter half of his life revealing the fascinating history of the town. His collection of articles, stories, and captivating facts can be found on the Colebrook Historical Society website.
